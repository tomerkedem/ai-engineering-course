Classical music has shaped musical culture for hundreds of years. It includes works written for orchestras, chamber groups, piano, opera, and many other forms. Composers such as Bach, Mozart, Beethoven, and Tchaikovsky created pieces that are still performed around the world. Classical music is often built around structure, harmony, melody, and development of musical themes. Unlike many modern songs that follow a short repeated format, classical compositions can be long and complex, moving through different moods and sections. Musicians who perform classical music usually spend years studying technique, reading notation, and interpreting the intentions of the composer. Although some people think classical music belongs only in concert halls, it continues to influence film scores, video game music, and popular culture. Many modern composers borrow ideas from classical traditions while combining them with electronic sounds and contemporary styles. This makes classical music both historic and still relevant today.
