Jazz music began as a rich blend of cultural traditions, rhythm, improvisation, and personal expression. It developed strongly in the United States and became one of the most influential musical styles of the twentieth century. Jazz is known for swing, syncopation, blue notes, and the freedom musicians have to improvise during a performance. Unlike music that is played exactly the same every time, jazz often changes from one performance to another. A saxophone player, pianist, trumpet player, or drummer may take a familiar melody and create new musical ideas in the moment. This makes jazz feel alive and conversational. Over time, jazz evolved into many styles, including bebop, cool jazz, fusion, and modern experimental forms. It influenced rock, pop, hip hop, and film music. Jazz also became a symbol of creativity because it rewards listening, response, risk, and collaboration between musicians on stage.
